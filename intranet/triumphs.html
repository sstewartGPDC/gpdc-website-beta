Triumphs in the Trenches

Defenders accrue a wealth of hard-won experience and well-earned victories. GPDC is spotlighting the against-all-odds victories of our public defenders from across the state. Share your story and inspire your colleagues.

Why Share Your Victories?

Every victory matters. Whether it's a not guilty verdict, a suppression motion granted, charges dismissed, or a sentence far below what the State requested, your success stories deserve to be celebrated and shared.

Sharing triumphs helps build a culture of excellence and reminds us all why we do this work. Your story could inspire a colleague facing a similar case or demonstrate to the public the vital role public defenders play in our justice system.

Thank you for sharing your triumph! We'll review your submission and be in touch soon.

Murder Case - Not Guilty After Trial

Not Guilty

Client was charged with felony murder after a fatal shooting. Through extensive investigation and expert testimony on self-defense, the jury returned a not guilty verdict after deliberating for less than two hours.

Felony Atlanta Circuit January 2026

DUI Suppression - Key Evidence Excluded

Motion Granted

Successfully argued that the traffic stop lacked reasonable suspicion. The court suppressed the breath test results and field sobriety tests, leading to dismissal of DUI charges.

Misdemeanor Cobb Circuit December 2025

Juvenile Transfer Hearing - Case Kept in Juvenile Court

Transfer Denied

15-year-old client faced transfer to adult court for armed robbery. Through mitigation evidence and adolescent brain development testimony, the court denied the State's motion to transfer.

Juvenile DeKalb Circuit November 2025
