APPLYING FOR REPRESENTATION

Applying for Attorney Representation

Learn about eligibility requirements and how to apply for public defender services.

Qualifications

Under Georgia law, you may qualify for a public defender if you demonstrate financial need through:

  • Inability to afford private counsel
  • Receipt of public assistance
  • Unemployment status
  • Earnings below 150% of the federal poverty level

Where to Apply

The application process varies by county. Generally, you can apply through:

  • Your local public defender office
  • The court where your case is pending
  • At your first court appearance

Understanding the Costs

Public defender services include:

  • A one-time $50 application fee (may be waived)
  • Potential post-conviction attorney fees based on income
  • Fee waivers available for those who qualify

WHAT TO EXPECT

Working With Your Public Defender

When you are assigned a public defender, you can expect dedicated legal representation throughout your case. Your attorney will:

  • Meet with you to discuss your case
  • Investigate the charges against you
  • Explain your legal options and rights
  • Represent you at all court appearances
  • Negotiate with prosecutors on your behalf
  • Prepare and present your defense at trial if needed

FOR FAMILIES

Supporting Your Loved One

Client Confidentiality

Please understand that attorneys are ethically obligated to maintain client confidentiality and are unable to discuss case details with family members without their client's explicit consent.

Attend Court

Your presence in court shows support and can positively impact your loved one's case. Dress appropriately and arrive on time.

Gather Documents

Help collect documents that may assist the case, such as employment records, character references, or proof of residence.

Maintain Contact

Regular communication with your loved one provides emotional support during a difficult time.
