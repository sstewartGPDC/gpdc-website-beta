ESTABLISHED 2003

Building the Foundation of Justice

The Georgia Public Defender Council was created by the Georgia General Assembly in 2003 to provide oversight, support, and resources for public defense throughout the state. Our foundation is built on the belief that every person deserves quality legal representation, regardless of their ability to pay.

We work to ensure that the constitutional right to counsel is not just a promise, but a reality for all Georgians. Through training, support services, and systemic advocacy, we strengthen the public defense system across our 45 circuit offices statewide.

OUR HISTORY

Two Decades of Service

2003

GPDC Established

The Georgia General Assembly creates the Georgia Public Defender Council to oversee and support indigent defense services statewide.

2005

Circuit System Implemented

The circuit public defender system begins operations, bringing organized public defense across Georgia's judicial circuits.

2010

Training Division Expanded

GPDC expands its training division to provide comprehensive professional development for public defenders statewide.

2015

Mental Health Advocacy

The Office of Mental Health Advocacy is established to provide specialized support for clients with mental health needs.

2020

Digital Transformation

GPDC implements JCATS case management system and expands virtual training capabilities.

Today

Continued Excellence

GPDC continues to support over 500 attorneys and staff serving clients across Georgia's 159 counties.
