About the Division

Empowering Clients Toward Stability and Success

Our Mission

We believe every person has inherent strengths that can be built upon for lasting change. By addressing housing, mental health, substance use, employment, and education needs, we help clients achieve stability that reduces recidivism and improves outcomes for families and communities.

Statewide Reach

Our team serves clients in every judicial circuit in Georgia, providing services both in-person and virtually. Whether in metropolitan Atlanta or rural South Georgia, our social workers are accessible to every eligible client referred by a Public Defender.

Leadership

Under the co-management of Catia De Los Santos (Client Support Services) and Kenya Holmes (Ladders Youth Program), the division has expanded its reach with decades of combined social work expertise.

Funding & Independence

All programs are provided at no cost to clients. The division is funded through state appropriations to the Georgia Public Defender Council and supported by the Georgia Public Defender Foundation.

Our Approach

What We Do

Our social workers provide integrated case management lasting 3-12 months, working alongside Public Defenders to create personalized strategies that address each client's unique circumstances.

We take a holistic, strengths-based approach — recognizing that each client has inherent capabilities that can be built upon for lasting change.

Needs & Strengths Assessments

Personalized evaluations identifying challenges and building on existing strengths.

Each client receives a comprehensive evaluation covering mental health, substance use, housing stability, employment readiness, family dynamics, and community connections. This assessment forms the foundation of your individualized case plan.

Individualized Case Plans

Custom plans tailored to address specific needs and set achievable goals.

Working collaboratively with you and your Public Defender, we develop a step-by-step plan with measurable milestones. Plans are reviewed monthly and adjusted based on your progress, changing needs, and input from your legal team.

Community Resource Referrals

Connections to housing, treatment, employment, and other local services.

We maintain partnerships with over 200 community organizations statewide — from housing authorities and treatment centers to job training programs and food assistance. We don't just refer; we follow up to ensure you're connected and supported.

Progress Monitoring

Ongoing tracking to ensure you stay on track with rehabilitation goals.

Regular check-ins, outcome tracking, and court reporting keep everyone aligned. We celebrate your wins and troubleshoot obstacles in real-time, ensuring continuous momentum toward your goals.

Eligibility

The Process

How It Works

1

Referral

Your Public Defender identifies that you may benefit from social services and submits a referral.

Referrals can come at any stage of your case — pre-trial, during proceedings, or post-disposition. Your defender knows your situation best and will recommend services when the time is right.

2

Assessment

A social worker conducts a comprehensive needs and strengths assessment.

This initial meeting typically lasts 60-90 minutes and covers all aspects of your life circumstances. Everything shared is confidential and protected by attorney-client privilege.

3

Case Plan

Together, we create an individualized plan with specific, achievable goals.

Your case plan becomes a living document — reviewed and updated monthly. Goals are broken into manageable milestones so you can track your progress clearly.

4

Active Support

3-12 months of ongoing case management, resource referrals, and monitoring.

During this phase, you'll have regular check-ins with your social worker — weekly at first, then as often as needed. We coordinate with your legal team, service providers, and support network.

✓

Transition

Successful transition with connections to continued community resources.

Graduation doesn't mean goodbye. We ensure you have lasting connections to resources, support groups, and community organizations that will continue to support your success.
