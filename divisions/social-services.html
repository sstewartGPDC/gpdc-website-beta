What We Do

Our social workers provide integrated case management lasting 3-12 months, working alongside Public Defenders to create personalized strategies that address each client's unique circumstances and promote long-term rehabilitation.

We take a holistic, strengths-based approach, recognizing that each client has inherent capabilities that can be built upon for lasting change.

Client-Centered
Strengths-Based
Holistic Approach

Eligibility

  • Must be 16 years or older
  • Must be referred by a Georgia Public Defender
  • Private attorney clients not eligible
01

Needs & Strengths Assessments

Personalized evaluations to identify each client's unique challenges and build on existing strengths.

02

Individualized Case Plans

Custom-developed plans tailored to address specific needs and set achievable goals.

03

Community Resource Referrals

Connections to essential local services including housing, treatment, and employment.

04

Progress Monitoring

Ongoing tracking and support to ensure clients stay on track with rehabilitation goals.

05

Post-Service Connections

Continued support and resource connections after case management concludes.

1

Referral

Your Public Defender identifies that you may benefit from social services support and submits a referral on your behalf.

2

Assessment

A social worker conducts a comprehensive needs and strengths assessment to understand your unique situation.

3

Case Plan Development

Together, we create an individualized case plan with specific, achievable goals tailored to your circumstances.

4

Active Support

Over 3-12 months, receive ongoing case management, resource referrals, and progress monitoring.

Transition & Follow-Up

We help you transition successfully with connections to continued community resources and support.

What is the Ladders Program?

The Ladders Program is a comprehensive 12-month development initiative designed specifically for justice-impacted youth ages 16-24. Through personalized support, education, and career training, we help participants build the foundation for a successful future.

Our fully virtual program makes services accessible statewide, with an additional 6 months of post-program support to ensure lasting success.

Fully Virtual
1-on-1 Support
18-Month Journey

Eligibility

  • Ages 16-24 years old
  • Prior or current justice involvement
  • Probation/parole acceptable
  • Cannot be currently incarcerated

Five Core Pillars

A comprehensive approach to building futures through these interconnected focus areas

01

Personalized Case Management

One-on-one specialists help set S.M.A.R.T. goals and build essential life skills for success.

02

Education Pathways

Free GED prep or vocational training through Georgia's Technical College System.

03

"Real Life, Real Talk" workshops on financial literacy, communication, and more.

04

Positive role models provide guidance and accountability throughout the program.

Job readiness training and employer connections for long-term career success.
